Assuming that DPS is calculated as: $$ DPS_N = \text{AS}_n \left(\text{AD}_n+\text{bonus}_{\text{AD}}\right) \left(1+\text{bonus}_{\text{AS}}\right) \left(1+\text{bonus}_{\text{CC}}\right) $$ where: $$AD_n = {base}_{AD} + (n-1) \cdot {perLV}_{AD}$$ $$AS_n = {base}_{AS} + (n-1) \cdot {perLV}_{AS}$$ $$AD = \text{attack damage}, AS = \text{attack speed}, CC = \text{critical hit chance.}$$

Assuming that a unit increase of each stat costs: $$cost_{\{AD,AS,CC\}} = \{36, 33 \cdot 100, 50 \cdot 100 \} $$ We have dps as a function of gold spent: $$DPS_{gold} = \text{AS}_n \left(\text{AD}_n+\frac{\text{gold}_{\text{AD}}}{36}\right) \left(1+\frac{\text{gold}_{\text{AS}}}{3300}\right) \left(1+\frac{\text{gold}_{\text{CC}}}{5000}\right) $$ Note that the DPS function increases monotonically, so it does not have any extrema.

Optimal gold allocation
